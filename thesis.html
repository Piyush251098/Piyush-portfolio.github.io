My master's thesis focuses on enhancing Conversational AI through Intent Recognition using LLMs and Generative AI.

The rapid advancement of Conversational AI systems has transformed human-computer interaction, enabling applications ranging from virtual assistants to customer support. However, accurately understanding and responding to user queries remains a significant challenge, particularly in identifying the under lying intent. This thesis explores intent recognition as a corner stone for enhancing conversational systems by utilizingLLMs and Generative AI.

Key Areas of Research:

    Taxonomy-based intent recognition.

    Query reformulation using AI models.

    Comparison of AI-driven vs traditional methods.

    Application in real-world chatbot assistants.
